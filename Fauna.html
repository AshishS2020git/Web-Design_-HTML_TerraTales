There are three types of fauna based on habitat:-
  1. Terrestrial Fauna
  2. Aquatic Fauna
  3. Aerial Fauna
Fauna keep the environment healthy, provide companionship to many in need and have guided this world through a list of catastrophic events. This only proves how crucial each and every one of their roles are, not just for the survival of the human species, but the entire planet.


Classification of Fauna:-
  • Mammals: Warm-blooded vertebrates with fur or hair, including humans, whales, and elephants.
  • Birds: Warm-blooded vertebrates with feathers, such as eagles, sparrows, and penguins.
  • Reptile: The most common example of this type is the Giant Sequoia Tree.
  • Fish: Aquatic vertebrates that have gills and fins, including sharks, lionfish, and tuna.
  • Invertebrates: Animals without a backbone, including insects, arachnids, mollusks, and crustaceans.
  • Amphibians: Cold-blooded vertebrates that typically undergo metamorphosis, such as frogs and salamanders.
