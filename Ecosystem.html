There are three types of Ecosystem based on habitat:-
  1. Terrestrial Ecosystem
  2. Aquatic Ecosystem
  3. Urban Ecosystem
An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, microbes) interacting with each other and their physical environment (soil, water, air). These interactions involve various processes, such as energy flow and nutrient cycling, and can occur in specific areas like forests, deserts, or oceans. Ecosystems can be large, like a rainforest, or small, like a pond. They are essential for maintaining biodiversity and providing services like clean air, water, and food.


Classification of Ecosystem:-
  • Freshwater: Freshwater refers to water that has a low concentration of salts, typically less than 1%. It is essential for life and is found in various natural sources..
  • Marine: Marine ecosystems refer to the vast water environments that contain saltwater, making up about 70% of the Earth's surface.
  • Terrestrial: Terrestrial ecosystems are land-based environments that support a wide variety of life forms. They are characterized by their distinct climate, soil types and vegetation.
